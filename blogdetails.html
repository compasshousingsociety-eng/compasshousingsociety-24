Waterfront condos: 2025 outlook & smart living

The harbour district is witnessing a steady appreciation as new smart-home technology becomes standard. From automated blinds to energy-efficient climate control, today's waterfront buyer expects seamless integration. We analyse price trends, inventory levels, and what developers are planning for 2026.

Market stability despite economic shifts

Contrary to predictions, waterfront condos have held firm with only a 1.2% dip in Q1, followed by a robust 3% rebound in Q2. Foreign buyer interest remains moderate, but domestic upgraders are driving demand for larger units with water views.

Smart home tech becomes baseline

Builders now include pre-wired sensor packages, voice-controlled lighting, and advanced security as standard. Resale units that lack these features often stay on the market 18 days longer. "It's not a luxury anymore – it's expected," says Compass analyst Laura Greer.

For investors, the rental pool remains strong – waterfront units command a 12% premium over similar inland properties. With several new towers breaking ground in the harbour east precinct, 2025 is shaping up as a pivotal year.

Financing and mortgage trends

With interest rate stabilization, first-time buyers are returning to pre-construction projects. Developers offer deposit structures that ease entry. However, pre-approval remains crucial. The post includes a downloadable checklist for buyers.
